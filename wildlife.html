Sri Lanka Wildlife Escapades

Sri Lanka is one of the finest places in the world where wildlife can be watched at its best. Sri Lanka might seem like a small droplet on the world map, but it encompasses some of its spectacular biodiversity hotspots. Sri Lankan Asian Elephants, Leopards, Sambar, and Purple-faced Langur are easy to spot here. Going on a safari is one of the best things to do in Sri Lanka.

Udawalawe national park

  • Location -sabaragamuwa and uva provinces
  • area -30.21k2
  • established-june 30,1972

Udawalawe national park, the sixth largest animal sanctuary of Sri Lanka has located 180km from Colombo in the south-central sector of the island. the main attraction of the park is the water buffalo, wild boar, spotted deer, jackal, mongooses, foxes, s the endemic toque macaque, and gray lingers. there are about 500 elephants in the park and they often roam in herds of up to 100. The best hours to visit the parking area are in the mornings and evenings. the late evening also affords the photographic opportunities in the backdrop of the loveliest sunset.

Kumana national park

  • Location -HMF9+5PJ, okandas
  • area -356.6km2
  • Management – Department of wildlife conservation
  • established - January 20, 1970

An unventured area to watch the beauty of wildlife and birdlife, this is a great place of natural beauty. Kumana park is best for bird-watching around the lagoons and tanks from April to July. Bird watchers can watch over 200 species easily. The park receives 1,300 millimeters of annual rainfall. Kumana National Park provides excellent feeding and resting habitats for many threatened wetland species.
